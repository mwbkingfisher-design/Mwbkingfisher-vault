“Finally! It has taken so much time to get here. But I've got to say. That's just beautiful,” said Bob. It was autumn, so the trees scattered around in the forest created a vivid and colorful view.

The whole forest was located in the valley surrounded by the sharp slopes. The only entrance led through a tight, dark tunnel. Jack stumbled upon it during one of his lone forest adventures.

But as his friends were looking at all this valley with amazement, it started to notice that something was not right. The water flew upwards. That wasn't a thing he would expect from the river. The fish were going against the current, which was also illogical.

The more he looked at his surroundings, the more strange things he noticed. The ape, which was supposed to hang onto trees and jump from one to another, dug a huge hole in the soil.

“Guys,” said Jack, “something is not right here; just look at that ape there. Shouldn't it hang on the trees rather than… I don't know, dig its own grave?”

But the guys? Amazement took control of their brains. They were like enchanted, and nothing could break that spell.

Bob was floating a few meters above the ground already. If everything here was opposite, then why not gravity?

Jack tried to get his friends down, but they were already too high. The spell didn't break even high above.

He tried to run to the entrance, but he started to float.

“Oh no…”
